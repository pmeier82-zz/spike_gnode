Extracellular recordings are well a established method in the field of electrophysiology used to analyze the activity of local populations of neurons. Voltage traces are recorded from tissue with microelectrodes, where the firing of neurons is visible as characteristic discharge patterns, so called spikes. The process of finding the times (detection) and estimating the number and identity of neurons (classification) from the discharge patterns in the recorded voltage traces is called spike sorting.

The accuracy of spike sorting can have a major impact on the quality of downstream analyses based upon the extracted spike data. Evidently, it is interesting to quantify how accurate a spike sorting algorithm works. It will allow those working with spike data in downstream analyses to quantify confidence in their input data, facilitate the development of better spike sorting methods and allow experimenter to take informed choices when searching for spike sorting methods for new projects.

Until now, there are no standard benchmark datasets or methods for evaluation, and those in existence have only been applied singularly. This service aims to break with that habit and wants to build a comprehensive performance landscape for the filed of spike sorting. To achieve that goal we rely on community contribution of datasets and spike sorting results. Results that users want to publish on this platform will then be available to the neuroscience community.
